JEHOVAH - YHVH

"JE HOVAH...is a mispronunciation of the Hebrew name, resulting from combining the consonants of that name, YHVH, with the vowels of the word Adonay , 'lord,' (Y-A-H-O-V-A-H), which the Jews substituted for the proper name in reading the scriptures. In such cases of substitution the vowels of the word which is to be read are written in the Hebrew text with the consonants of the word which is not to be read. The consonants of the word to be substituted are ordinarily written in the margin; but inasmuch as Adonay was regularly read instead of the ineffable YHVH, it was deemed unnecessary to note the fact at every occurrence. When Christian scholars began to study the Old Testament in Hebrew, if they were ignorant of this general rule or regarded the substitution as a piece of Jewish superstition, reading what actually stood in the text, they would inevitably pronounce the name Jehovah...

Several centuries before the Christian era the name YHVH had ceased to be commonly used by the Jews. Some of the later writers of the Old Testament employ the appelative Elohim, God, prevailingly or exclusively. A collection of Psalms (Ps. 42 to 83), was revised by an editor who changed the YHVH of the authors into Elohim...observe also the frequency of the 'Most High,' 'the God of Heaven,' "King of heaven,' in Daniel. The oldest Greek versions (Septuagint), from the third century B.C., consistently use 'Kirios' (Lord), where the Hebrew has YHVH, corresponding to the substitution of Adonay for YHVH in reading the original...

Josephus, who as a priest knew the pronunciation of the name, declares that religion forbids him to divulge it. (It will also be seen that Josephus was an initiate into the Christian mysteries); Philo calls it ineffable, and says that it is lawful for those only WHOSE EARS AND TONGUES are purified by Wisdom to hear and utter it in a holy place (that is, for priests in the temple); and in another passage, commenting on Leviticus 24:15,16, 'If any one, I do not say should blaspheme against the Lord of men and gods, but should even dare to utter his name unseasonably, let him expect the penalty of death.'...

22 Pathways...That they may be called Trees of Righteousness...(Isaiah 61:1-3).

In the last generations before the fall of Jerusalem, however, it was pronounced in a low tone so that the sound were lost in the chants of the priests." The Encyclopedia Judaica.

Spirit is the voice which rests on Bohu, and grasps it and guides it as required. This is symbolized in the words 'The Voice of the Lord is upon the waters,' (Psalm 29:3), and again, 'The Spirit of the Lord was hovering over the face of the waters.' (Genesis 1:2). By 'Face of the waters is meant 'a Stone' sunk in the abyss, so called because waters issue from it (the Navel of the Earth...see John 4:14; 7:38). Thus each was provided as befitted. Tohu is under the aegis of the name Shaddai; Bohu under the name Sabaoth; Darkness under the name Elohim, Spirit under that of YHVH.

Now we understand what happened to Elijah. There was a strong wind breaking the mountains, but the Lord was not in the wind. This is Shaddai. After the wind was a quaking, but the Lord was not in the quaking. This is Sabaoth. After the quaking there was a fire. But the Lord was not in the fire. This is Elohim from the side of Darkness. 'And after the fire was a still small voice.' And here at last was found the name YHVH. Zohar I.16a, 16b.
